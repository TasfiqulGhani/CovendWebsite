About COVID-19, The Coronavirus

Coronaviruses are a large family of viruses ranging from the common cold to severe diseases such as SARS-CoV in 2002, MERS-CoV in 2012 to the most recently discovered coronavirus SARS-CoV-2 better known as “COVID-19”

Since the first cases of acute respiratory syndrome were reported by the Chinese public health authorities in Wuhan City at the end of December in 2019, over 1.8 million cases have now been reported worldwide and increasing daily.

FAQ's

What happens WHEN I get infected ?


When a person is infected with COVID-19, the body’s response is to generate two antibodies (IgM / IgG) to help fight the infection. The earliest antibody produced is IgM in around 3~7 days in response to infection. In the later stage of infection IgG is produced and replaces IgM around 7 days after onset of symptoms. These antibodies fight infections by targeting specific antigens on the surface of the COVID-19 virus. You may or may not have symptoms.

How does the CoVend Test kit Work ?


The CoVend Test kit will detect either or both antibodies from a single drop of blood place into the test card. After a diluent is added, it flows along the nitrocellulose membrane (test strips) and binds to the anti-human IgM/IgG antibody to indicate your results. The CoVend Kit tests for the antibodies on dedicated strips giving it a higher accuracy.

What will my result show ?



• A negative result indicates you have not been recently exposed to the virus.
• Positive IgM only, indicates you are likely in the early stage of the virus.
• Positive IgM and IgG, indicates you are likely in the middle stage of the virus.
• Positive IgG only, indicates you are in the later stage of the virus.
The body’s response to COVID-19 from symptoms to viral load of IgM and IgG can vary in people.

If I test positive, what should I do?


Stay calm and isolate yourself from others. You need to contact your local governmental health organisation or your local doctor. Follow the recommenced guide lines which most likely will state to isolate for 7-14 days. If your symptoms are severe i.e difficulty in breathing, you may be admitted to hospital by your doctor.

If I test negative, what should I do?


Exercise caution and continue with good hygiene practices i.e washing your hand regularly. Keep 2 meters social distancing from others. In some cases the viral load has not reached a sufficient level (very early stage of infection). If you present symptoms a few days after your initial test, we would advise to take another test.

Can I get re-infected?


Information is still being gathered at the moment. There are some reports of reoccurring infections in Wuhan. With a second time infection they are speculating that the disease could be even harder for your system to fight off. Hence why it’s so important to know if you have been exposed already.

How accurate is the test ?


Laboratory studies have shown 100% accuracy. Clinically studies has shown specificity of 99.6%.

I have no symptoms and feel well, can the test detect COVID-19 ?


Yes. Regardless if you have symptoms or not, if you have had COVID-19, you will have had an immune response.

When will I get the result ?


Results will appear on the cassette after 15 minutes. If up to 20 minutes has passed, the test may not give you an accurate result. You should repeat the test again.

Is there a vaccine for COVID-19 ?


Currently there is no licenced vaccine for COVID-19

Is there a treatment for COVID-19 ?


Mild to moderate symptoms can be treated at home. However, if your symptoms get worse, you may need to contact your doctor and go to hospital.

What are the symptoms of COVID-19?


The most common symptoms of COVID-19 are a fever of 38C or above, a cough and shortness of breath. Symptoms can sometimes take from 2 and 14 days to appear. Those with such symptoms (about 80%) will make a full recovery within a few weeks without requiring hospital treatment.

Who is a most risk to becoming seriously ill from COVID-19 infections?


Those with underlying medical conditions (cancer, cardiovascular disease, chronic respiratory disease, diabetes) and older people are most likely to develop severe illness, pneumonia and acute respiratory distress syndrome as a result of having contracted COVID-19.

Has the CoVend test kit been approved ?

How is COVID-19 spread ?


COVID-19 spreads primarily through contact when an infected person sneezes or coughs. These droplets must then enter your nose, mouth or eyes either directly or indirectly when you touch a surface that has the virus.

How can I protect myself and my family?


You can protect yourself and your family by staying at home to help slow down the transmission. Practice social distancing when you leave home by avoiding close contact of at least 2 meters with others. Wash hand regularly with soap and water or hand sanitiser (containing alcohol of 60% or above). Cover your nose and mouth with your sleeve by flexing your elbow or use a tissue when you sneeze and cough. Avoid touching your face.
